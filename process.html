Digital Production Process:

Pre-production Asset Review

  • Development guidelines
  • Best practices
  • Media spec requirements
  • Creative file asset review
  • Studio checklist

Description: The project manager and production manager will work together to review relevant project documentation. This will include a discussion regarding the technical feasibility of the creative concept. More complex work will include the creative technology manager. The output of this step includes an out-of-pocket estimate, schedules for all deliverables and the identification of the appropriate development resources.

Resources: Project Manager, Production Manager, and Developer.

Development

  • Creative brief
  • Coding best practices
  • Studio guidelines
  • Brand Guidelines

Description: Upon approval of estimate and receipt of a RedWorks PO the project manager will coordinate a briefing or kick-off between the creative and development team. Creative direction will be provided and the developer will leverage coding best practices, studio and brand guidelines, to bring creative assets to life in the required digital format. The output of this step is the initial development and posting of the digital asset(s) to RedTrax for QC Review.

Resources: PM, Art Director, Production Manager, Creative Tech Manager, and Developer.

Quality Control

  • Comp layouts
  • Spec checker
  • Alt tags
  • URL/Clicktag Matrix
  • Render testing
  • Bug tracking/commenting

Description: Once the Quality Specialist receives notification from the developer that the asset is in QC Review they can begin testing. The Specialist will log any bugs in RedTrax as a task for the developer to complete. This cycle will continue until the asset passes QC Review. The output of this step is the QC approval of the digital asset in RedTrax, allowing the unit to advance to Internal Review.

Resources: Quality Specialist and Developer.

Internal and Client Review

Description: Once the PM and AD receives notification from the QC Specialist that the asset is in Internal Review they can begin reviewing. The PM will work with the AD to log any feedback in RedTrax as a task for the developer to complete. This cycle will continue until the the asset passes Internal Review. The output of this step is the Internal approval of the digital asset in RedTrax, allowing the unit to advance to Client Review. The same steps will be performed with PM/Account/Creative working closely with the client to log any feedback in RedTrax as a task for the developer to complete. The output of this step is the Client approval of the digital asset in RedTrax, allowing the unit to advance to QA Review.

Resources: PM, Account, AD, Client and Developer.

Quality Assurance

  • Confirmation of post-QC changes
  • Technical adherence of requirements (URLs, tracking, cicktags, media specs)
  • Multi-browser, multi-platform render testing

Description: Once the Quality Specialist receives notification from the PM that the asset is in QA Review they can begin testing. The Specialist will log any bugs in RedTrax as a task for the developer to complete. This cycle will continue until the asset passes QA Review. The output of this step is the QA approval of the digital asset in RedTrax, allowing the unit to advance to Final Review.

Resources: Quality Specialist and Developer.

Final Approval Delivery

  • Review of deliverables
  • Confirm required supporting assets are present
  • Approve for team to download for delivery

Description: The QA Specialist will notify the PM that the asset is ready for one last look before the final asset is downloaded from the system for delivery. This is the “gut check” to ensure everything is satisfactory. The output of this step is PM approval of the digital asset in RedTrax, allowing the unit to be downloaded from RedTrax for delivery. This step also informs the production manager that the work is complete and any dependent assets, like versions or resizes, can now begin and move into the production workflow.

Resources: PM and Production Manager.
